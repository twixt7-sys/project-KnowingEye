Chapter I

Introduction

Knowing Eye: A Full-Stack Session-Guided Web-Based Examination Platform with Integrated Behavior Monitoring — Legacy College of Compostela, Institute of Information Technology.

Background of the Study

The rapid shift toward computer-based and online assessment has exposed a persistent gap in academic institutions: existing examination systems lack reliable, real-time behavioral monitoring. As a result, institutions face increased cheating risks and an over-reliance on manual human supervision that does not scale. Knowing Eye addresses this gap by combining a centralized web-based examination platform with AI-powered behavioral monitoring built on computer vision and deep learning.

Problem Statement

Online examination systems lack reliable real-time behavioral monitoring, resulting in increased cheating risks and heavy reliance on manual supervision. Without automated, data-driven proctoring, examiners cannot objectively and consistently assess examination integrity across many concurrent sessions.

Proposed Solution

The study proposes a centralized web-based examination platform integrated with AI-powered behavioral monitoring using computer vision and deep learning. The platform captures webcam frames during a session, analyzes facial presence, gaze, posture, and identity in near real time, detects suspicious behaviors, and generates alerts and analytics for administrators.

Objectives of the Study

General objective. Design and implement a full-stack examination platform with real-time behavior monitoring.

Specific objectives.

  1. Build exam creation and management capabilities.
  2. Implement session-guided exam taking and answer tracking.
  3. Capture and log monitoring frames and session events.
  4. Analyze facial presence, gaze, and posture in near real time.
  5. Detect suspicious behaviors and generate alerts.
  6. Provide reporting and analytics for administrators.
  7. Support role-based access for examiners and examinees.

Scope and Delimitation

Included. Web-based exam management and delivery; role-based login for examiners and examinees; exam session tracking and response submission; behavior monitoring with webcam frame analysis; session logs, behavioral reports, and alerts; administrative dashboard and profile management.

Excluded. Standalone mobile application; external institutional SIS integration; specialized biometric hardware; nationwide deployment at this stage.

Significance of the Study

By automating proctoring and surfacing data-driven behavioral insights, Knowing Eye enhances examination integrity, reduces the supervision burden on administrators, and provides a transparent, reviewable audit trail. The system is designed to remain advisory — flagging behavioral indicators for human review rather than issuing definitive accusations.

Definition of Terms

  • ArcFace. A deep face-recognition model producing discriminative embeddings; used in the pipeline for examinee identity verification.
  • Behavioral monitoring. The continuous analysis of webcam frames to infer compliance indicators (face presence, gaze, posture).
  • Session. A single timed attempt of an exam by an examinee, tracked from start to submission.
  • WebSocket. A full-duplex communication protocol used to stream frames and broadcast alerts in real time.
  • YOLO. A real-time object-detection model family used for person and object (e.g., phone) detection.
