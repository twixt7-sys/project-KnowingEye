Chapter I

Background of the Study

Aligned with Draft Outline Paper — Introduction (pp. 1–5).

The use of digital technologies has changed how institutions conduct examinations, certifications, and competency assessments. Online and computer-based examination systems are widely used because they offer efficiency, scalability, and accessibility. However, as examinations shift to digital platforms, maintaining integrity, fairness, and reliability has become a major concern.

Globally, researchers have explored artificial intelligence and computer vision to improve monitoring through automated detection of behavior and identity verification — including systems such as iExam, AutoOEP, Talent-Interview, and related AI-based proctoring research cited in the draft outline.

At Legacy College of Compostela (LCC), detection of suspicious behavior still depends largely on CCTV monitoring or assigned proctors. During large-scale examinations this makes consistent monitoring difficult and increases proctor workload. Many existing systems focus on exam administration while providing only passive monitoring (e.g., webcam recording) without real-time behavioral analysis.

Proposed Solution

This study proposes Knowing Eye: A Web-Based Examination Platform with Behavior Monitoring Using Facial and Postural Analysis. The system integrates exam creation, administration, and monitoring into a single platform using computer vision and deep learning (YOLO, CNN feature extraction, FaceNet/ArcFace) to analyze head movement, eye gaze, and posture in real time and generate behavioral reports for administrators.

The study supports the CHED ACHIEVE Framework by promoting digital innovation and emerging technologies to address real-world challenges in examination environments.
