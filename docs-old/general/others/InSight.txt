modern days rather than just going with their intuition. This particular change is difficult for a lot of small to medium-sized businesses (SMEs), especially rental shops and boutique clothing stores since many of these businesses still rely on manual record-keeping methods like handwritten logbooks, spreadsheets, or simple sales tools. These methods may work for everyday transactions, however, they often make it hard to figure out how well sales are doing, what customers want, or what will happen in the future. Due to this, business owners have a hard time keeping track of their inventory, keeping customers, and running their businesses smoothly. Furthermore, recent studies have shown that data-driven decision-making is becoming more important for small to medium-sized businesses (SMEs). These studies have shown that using analytics is an essential factor in staying competitive and running a business efficiently [1].Globally, digital transformation has reshaped how organizations manage and interpret business information. Modern retail systems now combine sales monitoring, inventory tracking, and analytics within unified platforms that allow businesses to view their operations in real time. Advances in data analytics and machine learning have made it possible to extract meaningful insights from ordinary transaction records. Sales intelligence tools help identify purchasing patterns and improve stock management, while demand forecasting models assist businesses in preparing for seasonal changes and fluctuating customer needs [2], [3]. In addition, customer behavior analytics enables enterprises to better understand preferences and rental habits, supporting more informed and strategic planning. Studies show that predictive analytics significantly enhances retail decision-making by transforming transactional data into actionable business intelligence [4].Despite these developments, many SMEs at the national level continue to face barriers in adopting advanced technologies. Limited financial resources, lack of technical expertise, and reliance on familiar manual systems often slow the transition toward digital solutions. Large retail organizations benefit from sophisticated enterprise platforms, but smaller boutiques and rental businesses frequently lack systems designed specifically for their operational scale. Consequently, business decisions are often based on experience and manual observation rather than structured data analysis, placing smaller enterprises at a disadvantage in an increasingly competitive and data-driven market [1].Environmental scanning conducted for this study highlighted several operational concerns common among boutique rental businesses. The partner boutique experienced difficulty in efficiently tracking rental transactions because records were maintained manually through physical logbooks. Evaluating inventory status required manual counting before the owner could assess the condition of the business, which consumed time and increased the risk of inaccuracies. Locating past records proved equally challenging, as staff needed to search through multiple logbooks that occupied significant physical space. Furthermore, the boutique had no practical way to summarize operational data, requiring personnel to manually tally entries whenever reports or business evaluations were needed. These practices limited timely decision-making and prevented the effective use of accumulated business data for planning and improvement.Previous studies emphasize the benefits of integrated retail management systems in improving organizational efficiency by centralizing operations within a single digital platform. Research on sales intelligence systems highlights how automated reporting and real-time dashboards support faster and more accurate managerial decisions [4]. Similarly, machine-learning-based demand forecasting has demonstrated stronger predictive capability compared to traditional manual estimation methods, improving accuracy and reducing operational uncertainty [2], [5]. Customer analytics approaches further contribute by revealing behavioral patterns that allow businesses to anticipate customer needs instead of reacting after trends have already occurred [3]. However, many existing solutions are primarily designed for larger enterprises, leaving boutique-scale businesses with limited access to practical and affordable alternatives.Given these conditions, a clear gap emerges in the availability of an accessible platform that integrates rental management, sales intelligence, and predictive analytics specifically for boutique operations. Manual processes slow down monitoring activities, delay analysis, and increase the likelihood of human error. More importantly, valuable business data remains underutilized because it cannot easily be transformed into meaningful insights.To address these challenges, this study proposes InSight: A Data-Driven Sales Intelligence and Rental Management Platform with Machine-Learning-Based Customer Behavior Analytics and Demand Forecasting for Boutiques. The proposed system seeks to digitize rental and sales records, automate inventory monitoring, and provide analytical tools capable of identifying customer behavior patterns and forecasting product demand. Through the integration of machine learning techniques within a centralized platform, the system aims to convert routine operational data into actionable intelligence that supports informed and timely decision-making.The study supports the goals of the Commission on Higher Education’s ACHIEVE Framework by promoting innovation, digital transformation, and the development of industry-relevant technological solutions that contribute to local enterprise productivity. By applying data analytics and predictive modeling to real-world SME challenges, the research demonstrates how academic knowledge can be translated into practical solutions that benefit both businesses and the broader community.For clarity, several terms are defined within the context of this study. Sales intelligence refers to the analysis of sales data to guide strategic business decisions. Customer behavior analytics involves examining purchasing and rental patterns to better understand consumer preferences. Demand forecasting refers to predicting future product demand using historical data and machine learning techniques. Meanwhile, rental management systems are platforms designed to monitor item availability, rental transactions, and inventory status.Ultimately, this study responds to the growing need for accessible and intelligent retail management solutions. By bridging traditional boutique operations with modern data-driven technologies, the proposed platform aims to improve efficiency, strengthen decision-making processes, and support sustainable growth among boutique rental enterprises.REFERENCES:[1] S. The system aims to support boutique rental businesses by digitizing rental and sales operations and transforming operational data into analytical insights that assist managerial decision-making.The study is conducted with a selected boutique rental business that serves as the primary reference for system requirements and environmental scanning validation. The identified operational practices are used to represent common conditions experienced by small boutique and rental enterprises. The locale of the study is limited to the partner boutique where existing manual processes, including rental tracking, inventory monitoring, and record management, are currently performed using physical logbooks and manual tallying methods.Data gathered for the study include rental transaction records, sales data, inventory information, and customer transaction history obtained from existing boutique transaction records and system-generated operational datasets during testing and evaluation phases. These data are used solely for system development, analytics implementation, and performance validation.The population covered by the study consists of boutique personnel involved in daily operations, particularly business owners and staff responsible for rental recording, inventory monitoring, and report preparation. User interaction with the system is evaluated based on operational usability and functionality testing rather than large-scale customer deployment.The development period of the study spans the system analysis, design, implementation, testing, and evaluation phases conducted within the academic research timeline of the capstone project.The proposed system includes the following functional coverage:• Rental transaction management and tracking• Point-of-sale (POS) sales recording and inventory monitoring• Automated report generation and dashboard visualization• Customer behavior analytics based on historical transactions• Machine-learning-based demand forecasting using historical sales and rental dataThe study is limited to web-based implementation and focuses on analytics-driven decision support. Financial accounting integration, mobile application deployment, external enterprise system integration, and large-scale commercial deployment are beyond the scope of this research.---This study
