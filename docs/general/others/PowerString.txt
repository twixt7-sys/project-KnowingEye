electricity demand. As societies become more dependent on electronic devices, information systems, and automated environments, efficient energy management has become a critical concern for households, businesses, and public institutions. Effective monitoring of electricity consumption is essential not only for cost control but also for promoting sustainable energy usage and supporting environmental conservation efforts. Recent global energy reports emphasize the importance of improving energy efficiency through enhanced monitoring and data-driven management practices [1].Traditionally, electricity consumption monitoring relies primarily on periodic utility billing statements, which provide only summarized information after energy has already been consumed. While these records are useful for billing purposes, they do not offer real-time visibility into energy usage patterns or allow users to immediately identify excessive consumption. As a result, individuals and organizations often struggle to determine which activities or devices contribute most to energy usage, limiting their ability to implement timely corrective actions or optimize power consumption [5].Advancements in the Internet of Things (IoT) have introduced new opportunities for improving energy monitoring practices. IoT technologies enable interconnected devices equipped with sensors to continuously collect electrical parameters such as voltage, current, and power consumption and transmit data through network infrastructures for remote access and analysis. Studies on IoT-based energy monitoring systems demonstrate that real-time data acquisition allows users to observe consumption behavior as it occurs, improving awareness and supporting more responsive energy management decisions [2]. These systems transform traditional monitoring approaches from periodic observation into continuous and data-driven evaluation.Alongside IoT developments, machine learning techniques have increasingly been applied in energy management research to enhance analytical capabilities. Machine learning enables systems to analyze historical consumption data, identify recurring patterns, forecast future energy demand, and detect anomalies that may indicate inefficient usage or equipment irregularities. Predictive analytics provides proactive insights rather than reactive observations, allowing users to anticipate energy requirements and implement preventive strategies for efficiency improvement [3]. The integration of machine learning with energy monitoring platforms therefore represents a significant advancement toward intelligent energy management systems.At the national level, the Philippines continues to experience increasing electricity demand driven by population growth, expanding digital services, and economic development. Government initiatives such as the Energy Efficiency and Conservation Act promote the adoption of energy-saving technologies and encourage organizations to implement efficient energy monitoring practices [4]. Despite these initiatives, many households, educational institutions, and small offices still rely on conventional monitoring methods that provide limited analytical insight into real-time electricity consumption. The absence of accessible monitoring tools reduces opportunities for users to actively manage and optimize their energy usage.Environmental scanning conducted for this study identified operational practices commonly observed in local government and organizational environments. In several cases, including selected offices within the Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT), electricity consumption monitoring is performed manually through periodic checking of meter readings and review of billing statements. These practices lack centralized dashboards capable of aggregating and analyzing consumption data over time. Consequently, monitoring activities become time-consuming, trend analysis becomes difficult, and opportunities for identifying abnormal energy usage are often missed. These observations reflect broader challenges experienced by institutions that lack integrated and real-time energy monitoring solutions.Existing research highlights the effectiveness of IoT-based monitoring platforms in collecting real-time electrical data and presenting consumption information through digital dashboards. Previous systems successfully demonstrate remote monitoring capabilities and accurate measurement of electrical parameters using microcontroller-based solutions such as ESP32 devices. However, many implementations focus primarily on data collection and visualization while offering limited analytical intelligence or predictive functionality. Furthermore, commercially available smart energy monitoring solutions are often costly, limiting accessibility for small-scale users and public institutions.Given these conditions, a gap exists in the availability of an affordable and integrated energy monitoring platform that combines real-time IoT data acquisition with advanced analytics and predictive capabilities. Users require systems that not only display electricity consumption but also interpret data, identify usage trends, estimate costs, and detect unusual consumption patterns that may indicate inefficiencies.To address this need, this study proposes PowerString, a Machine-Learning-Driven IoT-Based Real-Time Energy Consumption Monitoring and Analytics System. The proposed platform aims to provide continuous monitoring of electrical parameters, centralized visualization of energy data, and analytical insights derived from historical consumption records. By integrating IoT sensing technologies with machine learning models for prediction and anomaly detection, the system seeks to transform raw electrical measurements into actionable information that supports informed energy management decisions.The study aligns with the Commission on Higher Education’s ACHIEVE Framework by promoting digital innovation, technological research, and industry-relevant solutions that contribute to sustainable development and smart infrastructure initiatives. Through the application of IoT and data analytics in real-world energy management contexts, the research demonstrates how academic innovation can support efficiency improvements within households, educational institutions, and government organizations.For clarity, several terms are defined within the context of this study. Internet of Things (IoT) refers to interconnected devices capable of collecting and transmitting data through networks. Energy monitoring systems are platforms designed to measure and analyze electrical consumption parameters. Machine learning refers to computational methods that enable systems to learn patterns from historical data to generate predictions or detect anomalies. Real-time monitoring describes the continuous observation of energy consumption as data is generated.Ultimately, this study responds to the growing demand for accessible and intelligent energy monitoring solutions. By combining real-time sensing, data analytics, and predictive modeling, the proposed system aims to improve energy awareness, support efficient consumption practices, and encourage data-driven energy management across various sectors.REFERENCES:[1] International Energy Agency, “Electricity Market Report 2023.” [Online]. Sensor data are transmitted via WiFi to a centralized backend server where the information is stored, processed, and analyzed. A web-based dashboard allows users to remotely monitor electricity usage through computers or mobile devices.The study covers the development and integration of the following system components:• Real-time acquisition of electrical parameters using IoT sensors• Wireless transmission of energy consumption data to a centralized server• Data storage and management using a relational database• Visualization of electricity consumption through an interactive web dashboard• Energy consumption analytics including usage trends and estimated electricity cost calculations• Application of machine learning techniques for energy consumption prediction and anomaly detection based on historical dataThe dataset used in the study consists of electrical measurements collected from connected monitoring hardware during system operation and testing phases. Historical records generated by the system serve as training data for analytical and predictive models.The study is limited to monitoring and analytical functions and does not include automated control of electrical appliances or integration with utility company billing infrastructures. System accuracy depends on proper sensor calibration and stable hardware operation. Machine learning predictions are limited by the quantity and quality of collected historical data, particularly during early deployment stages.Implementation is intended for small-scale environments such as households, offices, educational institutions, and small establishments. Large-scale industrial deployment and commercial production are beyond the scope of this research.---Objectives of the StudyThis study aims to develop PowerString, a machine-learning-driven IoT-based real-time energy consumption monitoring and analytics system capable of monitoring electrical usage, analyzing consumption patterns, and generating predictive insights to support data-driven energy management.Specifically, this study seeks to:Design and develop an IoT-based energy monitoring system capable of collecting electrical parameters including voltage, current, power, and energy consumption using sensor-integrated microcontroller devices.Implement a wireless data transmission mechanism that sends real-time electrical measurements from the monitoring device to a centralized server through network connectivity.Develop a web-based dashboard that enables users to:3.1 Monitor real-time electricity consumption3.2 View historical energy usage records3.3 Visualize consumption trends through graphical analyticsApply data analytics techniques to analyze collected electrical data in order to:4.1 Identify energy consumption patterns4.2 Determine peak usage periods4.3 Estimate electricity cost based on recorded consumptionIntegrate machine learning algorithms for intelligent energy analysis by:5.1 Predicting future energy consumption using historical data5.2 Detecting abnormal or unusual electricity usage patternsGenerate automated analytical reports and insights that assist users in understanding and improving their energy consumption behavior.Evaluate the functionality and usability of the developed system in supporting real-time monitoring and data-driven energy management for small-scale environments such as households and offices.
