Chapter I

Synthesis

Aligned with Draft Outline Paper 2 — Synthesis (p. 14).

The reviewed literature and related studies indicate that artificial intelligence and computer vision have significantly improved the security and reliability of online examination systems. Technologies such as facial recognition, eye gaze estimation, head pose detection, posture analysis, and object detection have been shown to effectively monitor examinee behavior and support academic integrity during remote assessments. Furthermore, studies demonstrate that combining multiple behavioral indicators provides more accurate and reliable monitoring than using a single detection method.

Despite these advancements, existing systems often focus on selected monitoring features or require complex deployment environments. There remains a need for an accessible web-based examination platform that integrates facial and postural analysis into a unified behavior monitoring system capable of operating in real time using standard webcams.

At Legacy College of Compostela (LCC), environmental scanning revealed that the institution does not currently utilize a computer-based examination system with integrated face tracking and identity verification. Suspicious behavior detection still depends on CCTV monitoring or assigned proctors, which makes consistent monitoring difficult during large-scale examinations and increases proctor workload. Many available platforms emphasize exam administration—question management and automated scoring—while offering only passive monitoring features such as webcam recording or screen locking, without real-time behavioral analysis of gaze direction, posture, or facial movement.

This study aims to develop Knowing Eye: A Web-Based Examination Platform with Behavior Monitoring Using Facial and Postural Analysis. By integrating exam creation, administration, and automated behavioral monitoring within a single web-based platform, the system is expected to enhance examination integrity, assist instructors in monitoring examinees more effectively, and provide a practical solution for secure online assessments. The platform applies computer vision and deep learning techniques—including YOLO for detection, CNN for feature extraction, and ArcFace for facial verification—to analyze head movement, eye gaze, and posture in real time, generating behavioral reports that support administrator review rather than replacing human judgment.
